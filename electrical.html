Electrical System Architecture

The BoxBot electrical system is designed for high-torque reliability and modular troubleshooting, featuring a dual-rail power architecture.

Figure 1: High-level System Wiring Diagram.

POWER MANAGEMENT

24V Primary Rail with integrated safety fusing.

  • Battery: 24V LiPo for high-current draw.
  • Protection: 6-circuit LED blade fuse block.
  • Conversion: Buck converters for 5V logic stability.

LOGIC & CONTROL

Raspberry Pi 2 and Arduino Serial interface.

  • Primary Controller: Raspberry Pi 2 for Vision/Logic.
  • MCU: Arduino for low-level motor pulsing.
  • Sensors: RealSense depth camera and limit switches.

ACTUATION

High-torque Sabertooth 2x12 motor drivers.

  • Drive Motors: Dual-channel output for U-shape drivetrain.
  • Precision: PWM-controlled variable speed scaling.
  • Connectors: Vibration-resistant XT60 connectors.

Major Design Decisions

Standardization vs. Customization

We chose the Sabertooth 2x12 over custom H-bridge circuits to prioritize reliability and familiarity from previous projects. This allowed us to focus our development time on autonomous sensor fusion rather than troubleshooting power-stage failures.
