Mechanical Design & CAD

This is the CAD of BoxBot. We decided that we wanted the drivebase to be as low to the ground as possible, squeeze the box with with a rack and pinion system, and lift up the box with a scissor lift mechanism.

Full Assembly CAD

1. Drive Base & Chassis

The foundation consists of an aluminum baseplate supporting four 72mm Omni Wheels. This configuration enables holonomic movement for precise box alignment. Stability is augmented by roller ball transfer bearings positioned at key balance points.

2. Scissor Lift Mechanism

Two symmetrical scissor lifts provide vertical actuation. These are powered by 25kg high-torque digital servos. The mechanism utilizes linear ball bearings to maintain smooth vertical travel during the lifting phase.

3. Center Plate & Power Housing

The center plate acts as the structural bridge between the two drive pods. It houses the 24V 4Ah battery, positioned centrally to optimize the center of gravity. Electronics, including the Raspberry Pi 2 and Sabertooth driver, are mounted here for protection.

4. Rack and Pinion Lateral Adjustment

To accommodate various box sizes, a rack and pinion system provides lateral movement. It features a 4ft metal gear rack with a 14-1/2 degree pressure angle and 32 pitch. This allows the arms to slide precisely along 8mm stainless steel rods.
